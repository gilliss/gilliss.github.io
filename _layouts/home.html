Brief Bio
I am a PhD student in UNC's Experimental Nuclear and Astroparticle Physics (ENAP) group. My graduate work focuses on background modeling, Monte Carlo simulation, signal processing, and detector construction for germanium-based neutrinoless double-beta decay experiments. My dissertation applies Bayesian statistical modeling and MCMC techniques to spectral measurements. I am a Science Ambassador at the Morehead Planetarium and Science Center, and I hold a bachelors degree in physics from The College of Wooster.
