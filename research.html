Research Projects

  • Text Simplification

    Undergraduate Thesis Project

    Guide : Prof. Pushpak Bhattacharya, IIT Bombay

    • Working on text simplification and enhancement of readability of text to aid children, illiterate, non-native English learners, disabled (aphasic) people read and understand text. We are mainly focussing on lexical simplification, which has received lesser attention compared to its counterpart - syntactic simplification of complicated sentence structures.
    • We are trying to model the context of a word occurrence better, so as to improve the replacement accuracy.
    • We are also looking at paraphrasing of complex words using simpler ones and not limited by synonymy.

  • Temporal Analysis of Sentiment Events

    Undergraduate Research & Development Project

    Guide : Prof. Pushpak Bhattacharya & Prof. Saketha Nath J, IIT Bombay

    • Working on exploring the temporal dimension of sentiment analysis.
    • We are mainly involved with identifying major sentiment or opinion shift on a particular event or campaign over time.
    • We majorly concentrate on social media, \emph{twitter} in particular, to extract sentiment expressions about a particular event in terms of tweets.
    • We also try to find other interesting factors with regards to the event/campaign such as most influential tweeter (market mavens), most impactful event.
Research Internships

  • Automated Linguistic Personalization of Ad Messages

    Guide : Rishiraj Saha Roy, Adobe Research Labs, Bangalore

    • Designed and developed a novel method of personalizing ad messages based on the linguistic style of a target segment
    • Audience style represented using language models estimated from user generated textual content on social media and evaluated using cross-entropy
    • Extracted appropriate modifiers from positive sentiment advertisements and inserted them in context for automatic transformation of ads for selected product
    • Crowdsourced experiments performed to verify indistinguishability from human generated ads
    • Research paper (long) submitted to WSDM 2015 Conference

  • Synthesis Modulo Bisimulation of Distributed Automaton Systems

    Guide : Prof. Madhavan Mukund, Chennai Mathematical Institute

    • We are working on the open problem of synthesizing distributed implementation from global specifications in the framework of transitionsystems, using bismulation as the equivalence criterion
    • We focussed on the loosely cooperating model of distributed transition systems
    • The synchronization does not involve any communication of local information between the processes
    • We have gathered evidence that the collapse in the state space that occurs when one takes the bisimulation quotient of a distributed transition system can be quantified
